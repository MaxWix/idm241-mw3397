What is a Microinteraction and why?

What is a Microinteraction?

  • Microinteractions are interactive events that involve one purpose and task. They bring engaging and delightful moments to users, humanizing an otherwise very inhuman environment. Microinteractions are bountiful throughout all websites.

What makes up a Microinteraction?

  1. Triggers are what initiate a microinteraction. Examples of triggers could be on-hover, on-click, page-load, on-scroll, etc.
  2. Rules determine what happens upon a microinteractions trigger, these rules can provide feedback to the user and/or can be back-end reactions
  3. Feedback is what lets the user know about a microinteraction that is happening. It is what the user sees, feels, or hears after triggering a microinteraction.
  4. Loops and Modes involve microinteractions that change conditions in an environment, this might involve looping an animation or causing a modal to open.

Why are microinteractions important?

  • Meaningful microinteractions are essential to the production of a successful digital product. They communicate the happenings and inner workings of a product to the user and humanize the process.
